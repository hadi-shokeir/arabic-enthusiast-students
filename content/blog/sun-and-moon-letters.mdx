## What Is the Rule?

When the definite article **ال** (al-) is placed before a noun, one of two things happens:

1. The **lām** (ل) is **silent** — the first letter of the noun is doubled instead.
2. The **lām** (ل) is **pronounced** — exactly as written.

Which one depends entirely on the first letter of the noun.

## Sun Letters (الحروف الشمسية)

These are the 14 letters that **assimilate** the lām. When a word begins with one of these, the lām disappears into the following letter:

| Letter | Example | Pronunciation |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| ت | التَّوْبَة | at-tawba (not al-tawba) |
| ث | الثَّلْج | ath-thalj |
| د | الدِّين | ad-dīn |
| ذ | الذَّهَب | adh-dhahab |
| ر | الرَّحْمَن | ar-raḥmān |
| ز | الزَّمَان | az-zamān |
| س | السَّلَام | as-salām |
| ش | الشَّمْس | ash-shams (the sun itself!) |
| ص | الصَّلَاة | aṣ-ṣalāh |
| ض | الضَّوء | aḍ-ḍaw' |
| ط | الطَّرِيق | aṭ-ṭarīq |
| ظ | الظُّهْر | aẓ-ẓuhr |
| ل | اللَّيْل | al-layl (lam doubles) |
| ن | النُّور | an-nūr |

## Moon Letters (الحروف القمرية)

The remaining 14 letters **keep** the lām pronounced. These are easier — no change:

| Letter | Example | Pronunciation |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| ا | الْأَرْض | al-arḍ |
| ب | الْبَيْت | al-bayt |
| ج | الْجَمَال | al-jamāl |
| ح | الْحَمْد | al-ḥamd |
| خ | الْخَيْر | al-khayr |
| ع | الْعِلْم | al-ʿilm |
| غ | الْغَيْب | al-ghayb |
| ف | الْفَتْح | al-fatḥ |
| ق | الْقُرْآن | al-qur'ān |
| ك | الْكِتَاب | al-kitāb |
| م | الْمَسْجِد | al-masjid |
| ه | الْهُدَى | al-hudā |
| و | الْوَقْت | al-waqt |
| ي | الْيَوْم | al-yawm |

## The Easy Way to Remember

**Sun letters** are all the letters that are articulated near the front of the mouth — at or near the same place as the lām itself. So your tongue naturally slides into the next letter rather than stopping.

**Moon letters** are articulated further back, or use different articulation points — so the lām stays distinct.

> **Pro tip:** The word الشَّمْس (ash-shams, "the sun") starts with a sun letter — ش. And الْقَمَر (al-qamar, "the moon") starts with a moon letter — ق. The rule named itself.

## Why Does This Matter?

This rule is **everywhere** in Arabic. Every time you read a word with ال, you're applying it. In Quranic recitation (Tajweed), it is non-negotiable. In everyday speech, native speakers apply it automatically — and mispronouncing it marks you as a learner.

Once you internalize which letters are which, your reading fluency improves dramatically because you stop second-guessing every ال you see.
